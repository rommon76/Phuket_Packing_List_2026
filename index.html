Phuket-specific tips

▾
  • July = wet season — warm (28–32°C days), humid (70–85%), expect an afternoon shower most days. Mornings are usually sunny — plan outdoor activities early.
  • Currency = Thai Baht (THB). ~AUD $1 ≈ 22–24 THB. ATMs everywhere but charge 220 THB per withdrawal — take out larger amounts fewer times. Card widely accepted at hotels/malls, cash needed for street food, markets, tuk-tuks.
  • Tipping is NOT expected but appreciated. 20–50 THB for hotel porters, round up taxis, 10% at nicer sit-down restaurants if service isn't already added.
  • Do NOT drink the tap water. Bottled water for drinking + brushing teeth. Hotel provides refill jugs. Use a filter bottle (Grayl / LifeStraw) if you have one.
  • Temple dress code (Big Buddha + Wat Chalong, Day 5): cover shoulders + knees. Sarongs available on-site but faster if you dress appropriately from the start.
  • Getting around: tuk-tuks (~200–400 THB short trips, agree price before), Grab app (like Uber, cheaper + metered), hotel taxis. Avoid airport tuk-tuks — use pre-booked minivan or metered taxi at official rank.
  • Bangla Road etiquette: friendly and safe early evening (fine for teens with the family). Post-9pm gets louder + more adult — a 16-year-old can absolutely handle Bangla Rd with you, especially early evening. Watch drinks, agree prices at bars before ordering buckets.
  • Sun is intense. Even overcast days burn. Reef-safe SPF 50+ (Hawaii + Thailand marine parks require reef-safe now).
  • Time zone: Phuket is UTC+7 · 3 hours behind Sydney (in Sydney winter). No jet lag really — you'll adjust in an hour.
  • Bargaining is expected at markets (Jungceylon retail stores fixed price). Start at 40–50% of asked price, stay friendly, walk away is the strongest move.
  • Safety: Phuket is very safe overall. Watch for pickpockets in Bangla Road crowds, don't rent scooters if you're not experienced (locals ride hard).
